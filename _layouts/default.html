Nguyen Manh Tien Anh

I am currently an AI Engineer at Vingroup Big Data Institute, where I work on Speech Processing, Large Language Models (LLMs), Multimodal Learning, and Intelligent Agents, focusing on real-world applications such as smart virtual assistants for VinFast cars. I am advised in my current work by Dr. Kim Anh Nguyen and Dr. Van Huy Nguyen.

In 2023, I received my Engineer's degree in Information Technology from the School of Information and Communication Technology, Hanoi University of Science and Technology, where I completed my graduation thesis under the supervision of Assoc. Prof. Sami Souihi. My thesis, titled “Deep Learning in NLP for Anomalous HTTP Requests Detection,” explored the intersection of NLP and cybersecurity and was recognized as the Best Thesis in the cybersecurity committee.

From 2021 to 2025, I also worked as a Research Assistant at the LISSI Tincnet laboratory, Université Paris-Est Créteil (UPEC), focusing on NLP applications for network management and cybersecurity. These academic and industry experiences have shaped my strong interest in language variation, user modeling, and the development of adaptive, multimodal AI systems using LLMs.

Research Interests

My research interests lie at the intersection of Natural Language Processing and Speech Technologies. I focus on Large Language Models (LLMs), Speech Processing, Multimodal Learning, Intelligent Agents, and Cybersecurity. I am particularly interested in their applications to virtual assistants, where these technologies enable adaptive, context-aware, and secure human-computer interactions.

Publications

 Zero Trust: Deep Learning and NLP for HTTP Anomaly Detection in IDS
Journal Paper
Nguyen Manh Tien Anh, Van Tong, Sondes Bannour Souihi, and Sami Souihi
IEEE Journal on Selected Areas in Communications, Volume: 43, Issue: 6, June 2025

This paper presents a Zero Trust-based intrusion detection system that leverages NLP, Deep Learning, and Transfer Learning to accurately and efficiently detect both known and novel anomalous HTTP requests.
Improving speech recognition with prompt-based contextualized asr and llm-based re-predictor
Conference Paper
Nguyen Manh Tien Anh, and Ho Sy Thach
Interspeech, pp. 737-741, 2024

This paper proposes a novel architecture integrating Large Language Models (LLMs) and prompt mechanisms to significantly enhance Automatic Speech Recognition (ASR) accuracy in challenging conditions.
Deep Learning in NLP for Anomalous HTTP Requests Detection
Conference Paper
Nguyen Manh Tien Anh, Van Tong, Sondes Bannour Souihi, and Sami Souihi
19th International Conference on Network and Service Management (CNSM), 2023

This paper proposes a novel model combining NLP, DL, and the BERT model to differentiate normal from anomalous HTTP requests, achieving high accuracy on known threats and demonstrating transfer learning capability for unseen attack patterns.
When NLP meets SDN : an application to Global Internet eXchange Network
Conference Paper
Nguyen Manh Tien Anh, Sondes Bannour Souihi, Hai Anh Tran, and Sami Souihi
IEEE International Conference on Communications (ICC), 2022

This paper proposes an NLP-driven framework using a seq2seq LSTM model to enable natural language-based network configuration for Intent-Based Networking (IBN).
